Getting Started

Set up your API keys in under 5 minutes and start turning voice notes into structured meeting summaries.

What you need 2 API keys, both free to start

Service What it's used for Free tier Where to get it
Groq Transcribes your audio (speech → text) Free console.groq.com
Anthropic Summarises, identifies speakers & action items Pay as you go console.anthropic.com
💡
Typical cost per meeting A 30-minute meeting costs roughly $0.02–0.05 in Anthropic credits. Groq transcription is free on the standard tier. You'll only be charged for what you actually use — there are no subscriptions.
1

Get your Groq API Key free · takes ~2 min

1
Open your browser and go to:
https://console.groq.com
2
Sign up with Google or email. No credit card required.
3
In the left sidebar, click API Keys.
4
Click Create API Key. Give it any name (e.g. "MeetingMind").
5
Copy the key — it looks like this:
gsk_xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx
⚠ Copy it now — it won't be shown again after you close the dialog.
2

Get your Anthropic API Key pay-as-you-go · ~$5 credit to start

1
Open your browser and go to:
https://console.anthropic.com
2
Sign up with Google or email.
3
You'll be asked to add a payment method and load credits. $5 is more than enough for hundreds of meetings.
4
In the left sidebar, click API Keys, then Create Key.
5
Copy the key — it looks like this:
sk-ant-api03-xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx
⚠ Copy it now — it won't be shown again after you close the dialog.
⚠️
Keep your keys private Never share your API keys with anyone or post them anywhere. They are linked to your billing account. MeetingMind stores them only in your own browser and your private account — no one else can see them.
3

Add your keys to MeetingMind one-time setup

1
Open MeetingMind and sign in with Google.
2
Click Settings (gear icon at the bottom of the left sidebar).
3
Paste your keys into the correct fields and make sure Groq is selected as the transcription provider.
4
Click Save. Your keys are stored privately — they're never visible to anyone else.

Link your Telegram optional · send voice notes on the go

If you want to send voice notes directly from Telegram and have them automatically appear in your MeetingMind dashboard, follow these steps.

1
In MeetingMind Settings, scroll to the Telegram bot section and click Link my Telegram.
2
A command will appear — copy it (it expires in 15 minutes).
3
Open Telegram, search for the bot, and send the command.
4
The bot replies "Account linked!" — you're done. Send any voice note to process it.
/start a1b2c3d4e5f6g7h8 ← example command, yours will differ
🔗
Your keys, your cost Once linked, the Telegram bot uses your Groq and Anthropic keys for every voice note you send. You are only charged for your own usage.
